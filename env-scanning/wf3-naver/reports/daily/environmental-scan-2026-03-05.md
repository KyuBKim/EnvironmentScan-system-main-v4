### 8.2 FSSF Classification Methodology

The Future Signal Scanning Framework (FSSF) classifies signals into 8 types based on their position in the signal-to-trend maturation lifecycle:

- **Weak Signal**: Early, ambiguous indicators of potential future change. Low current visibility but high future impact potential. (Example today: TerraPower nuclear permit, virtual cell models)
- **Emerging Issue**: Issues gaining recognition but not yet widely discussed or acted upon. (Example today: AI liability lawsuits, quantum AI)
- **Trend**: Established patterns of change with clear direction and supporting evidence. (Example today: Gangnam housing decline)
- **Megatrend**: Large-scale, sustained forces affecting all domains. (None detected today)
- **Driver**: Causal forces actively creating future conditions. (Example today: Judicial reform, NK missiles, data protection law)
- **Wild Card**: Low-probability, high-impact events that challenge assumptions. (Example today: Iran-Turkey attack, Kurdish invasion)
- **Discontinuity**: Fundamental breaks from previous patterns. (Example today: Hormuz insurance 12x surge)
- **Precursor Event**: Observable events that may herald larger systemic changes. (Example today: KOSPI 10% swing)

Three Horizons classify signals by when their primary effects materialize: H1 (0-2 years, current regime), H2 (2-7 years, transitional), H3 (7+ years, future regime).

Tipping Point detection uses two indicators: Critical Slowing Down (system approaching phase transition, recovery time increasing) and Flickering (system oscillating between states at increasing amplitude).
