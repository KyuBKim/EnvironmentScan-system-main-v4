### 8.2 FSSF Classification Methodology

The FSSF (Future Signal Scanning Framework) classification system categorizes signals into 8 types based on their nature, certainty, and temporal dynamics:

- **Weak Signal**: Early indicators of potential change with high novelty but low current visibility. Often found in specialized or non-mainstream sources. Example: KAIST planetary boundary research using annual emission methodology.
- **Emerging Issue**: Issues gaining traction with growing evidence but not yet widely recognized. Still in formative stages. Example: AI industry bifurcation between military and civilian ecosystems.
- **Trend**: Established patterns of change with clear direction and momentum. Widely recognized and discussed. Example: Local election preparations, K-defense export growth.
- **Megatrend**: Large-scale, slow-moving transformations that reshape entire sectors or societies. Typically multi-decade dynamics. None detected today due to crisis-dominated coverage.
- **Driver**: Active forces (policy, regulation, technology) that directly cause or accelerate change. Example: Google app fee reduction, government price cap policy.
- **Wild Card**: Low-probability, high-impact events that disrupt existing assumptions and create radical uncertainty. Example: Hormuz blockade, NK naval nuclear test.
- **Discontinuity**: Threshold-crossing events that fundamentally alter system dynamics. Once crossed, previous assumptions no longer hold. Example: Oil breaking $80, GPT-5.4 release.
- **Precursor Event**: Events that signal larger changes ahead. Not impactful in isolation but indicative of emerging dynamics. Example: Presidential approval rating shift, K-battery simultaneous decline.
