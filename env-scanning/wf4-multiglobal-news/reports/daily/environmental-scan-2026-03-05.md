### 8.4 FSSF Classification Methodology

The Future Signal Scanning Framework (FSSF) 8-type classification system categorizes signals along two dimensions: information maturity (how established the evidence is) and impact potential (how transformative the change could be).

- **Weak Signal**: Low information maturity, potentially high impact. Early indicators that are not yet mainstream but could signal paradigm shifts. Examples today: China-Iran arms restraint, UK Green Party breakthrough, MIT protein design.
- **Emerging Issue**: Growing recognition but unclear trajectory. Issues gaining momentum in media and policy discourse. Examples: Chinese AI competition, nuclear deterrence cooperation, EU industrial policy.
- **Trend**: Established patterns with measurable momentum and directional clarity. Examples: US tariff escalation, eurozone recovery, presidential trial proceedings.
- **Megatrend**: Long-term structural forces operating across decades. Examples: global debt acceleration ($29T), climate change adaptation imperative.
- **Driver**: Active forces pushing change across multiple domains simultaneously. Example: oil price surge driving financial, political, and social changes.
- **Wild Card**: Low probability events with extreme impact -- especially powerful when realized. Example: Israel's Tehran strikes represent a Wild Card that has materialized, making its impact assessment critical.
- **Discontinuity**: Breaks in established patterns that indicate systemic regime change. Example: KOSPI's record crash exceeding 9/11 represents a financial discontinuity.
- **Precursor Event**: Events that signal potential larger developments ahead. Examples: European joint military statement, Iraq blackout, Japanese yen crisis -- each indicating possible escalation.
