### 8.4 FSSF Classification Methodology

The Future Signal Scanning Framework (FSSF) classifies signals into 8 types based on their maturity, predictability, and potential impact magnitude:

- **Weak Signal**: Early, ambiguous indicators of possible future change. Low visibility, high uncertainty. Requires pattern recognition across multiple sources. (Example today: Ocean level rise research revealing 30cm underestimation)
- **Emerging Issue**: Identifiable developments gaining momentum but not yet mainstream. Rising visibility, reducing uncertainty. (Example today: China fuel export halt)
- **Trend**: Established patterns with clear directional movement and quantitative support. (Example today: China 4.5-5% growth target)
- **Megatrend**: Large-scale, slow-moving forces that shape the macro environment over decades. (Example today: China five-year quantum/AI plan)
- **Driver**: Active forces that accelerate or enable future change. Often policy-driven. (Example today: Korea AI budget tripling to 10.1 trillion won)
- **Wild Card**: Low-probability, high-impact events that could fundamentally alter trajectories. (Example today: US-Israel strikes on Iran)
- **Discontinuity**: Sharp breaks from established patterns, often with quantitative thresholds crossed. (Example today: Hormuz traffic 90% drop)
- **Precursor Event**: Early-stage events that may precede larger shifts. Requires close monitoring for escalation. (Example today: Kurdish militia coalition formation)
