**Theme 3: Decentralization Under Stress**
Papers on DAO governance failures, prediction markets, and geopolitical modeling reveal that decentralized systems face scaling limits, with governance participation collapsing at capacity breakpoints. This challenges the Web3 narrative of unlimited scalable decentralization. The monitoring limits research specifically identifies endogenous concentration dynamics where initial decentralization efforts paradoxically lead to power concentration as participant monitoring capacity is exceeded. Combined with prediction market studies from the 2024 US Presidential Election, this suggests that decentralized decision-making systems face fundamental cognitive and organizational constraints that current designs do not adequately address.

**Theme 4: Computational Paradigm Shifts**
The convergence of diffusion language models breaking factorization barriers, 4-bit attention quantization enabling edge deployment, and MoE expert divergence learning improving capacity utilization signals a fundamental shift in how AI models are designed, trained, and deployed. These architectural innovations collectively suggest that the next generation of AI systems will be more efficient, more distributed, and less dependent on centralized compute infrastructure than current transformer-based models.
